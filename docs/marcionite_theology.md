# Marcionite Theology — Reference for VOTD Prompts

This document captures the doctrinal grounding the Verse of the Day reflections should reflect. Used as context for the LLM that writes daily blurbs.

## The Two Gods

The single most important Marcionite distinction. The **God of the Hebrew Bible** (the Demiurge, Creator, Yahweh, "the Judge") is **not the same** as the **Father of Jesus Christ** (the Stranger God, the God of pure mercy and goodness).

- The Demiurge is the god of wrath, judgment, legalism, and law.
- The Father is the god of grace, mercy, deliverance, and spirit.
- Christ revealed the Stranger God for the first time. The Old Testament does not progressively prepare for Him; it stands as contrast.
- The Law is a system of captivity that Christ rescues humanity from — not a covenant fulfilled.

## Christology

- Jesus the Christ was **not born of Mary**. He **descended** into the synagogue at Capernaum already fully grown in the 15th year of Tiberius Caesar.
- There is **no nativity**, no Bethlehem, no manger, no virgin birth, no childhood, no incarnation as a baby.
- Marcionites are **not docetists** — Christ was real, but his coming was a descent, not a birth.
- Christ's mission was **rescue from the Demiurge/Creator**, not the fulfillment of Hebrew prophecy.

## Paul's Gospel

- Only the Apostle Paul received the full and true revelation of Christ.
- Paul's gospel is a **new universal covenant**, not an extension of Judaism.
- Pauline contrasts (the essence of the gospel):
  - **Law vs. grace**
  - **Wrath vs. mercy**
  - **Works vs. faith**
  - **Flesh vs. spirit**
  - **Sin vs. righteousness**
  - **Death vs. life**
  - **Bondage vs. deliverance**
- The Petrine tradition (apostle Peter, the Jerusalem church) tried to keep Christianity tied to Jewish law. Marcionites reject this — Christians are not bound by Mosaic law, circumcision, dietary rules, or the Hebrew scriptures.

## What Marcionites Are NOT (Common Misattributions)

These doctrines are often falsely attributed to Marcionites by critics (especially Tertullian, who conflated Marcion with Cerdo):

- **Not dualists** — the Father is supreme; the Demiurge is lesser, not equal.
- **Not docetists** — Christ was real.
- **Not gnostic** — salvation is by grace through faith in Christ, not by secret knowledge.
- Do **not forbid marriage**.
- Do **not impose celibacy**.
- Do **not prohibit wine or meat**.

## Marcionite Priority

- The Evangelicon (the Gospel of the Lord) **predates** the canonical Gospel of Luke.
- Luke is a later expansion of the Evangelicon, redacted by Theophilus of Antioch around 169 CE to align with emerging Catholic theology.
- Marcion's Pauline epistles are closer to the originals than the canonical versions, which were interpolated by Catholic editors to make Paul appear aligned with Petrine teaching.

## The Testamentum

The first Christian Bible, compiled by Marcion:

- **Evangelicon** — the Gospel of the Lord Jesus Christ
- **Apostolicon** — the ten original Pauline epistles
- **Antilegicon** — Titus, 1 & 2 Timothy, Alexandrians (added later by Apelles)
- **Psalmicon** — Christian psalms
- **Homileticon** — Diognetus
- **Synaxicon** — letters of Ignatius and Metrodorus

## Marcionite Worship and Practice

- No use of the Hebrew scriptures in worship.
- No celebration of Christmas as a nativity (since there was no nativity).
- Easter is celebrated as the resurrection of Christ.
- Salvation is by grace through faith in Christ alone (though Apelles emphasized moral discipline as a fruit of faith).
- The Lord's Prayer and sacraments are practiced.
- Strong emphasis on Paul as the singular authoritative apostle.

## Key Historical Figures

- **Marcion of Sinope** (~70–154 CE) — bishop, ship-owner, compiled the Testamentum, martyred in the Colosseum.
- **Apelles** — Marcion's most prominent disciple, bishop of Alexandria, developed the angel-as-creator theology and works-based moral discipline.
- **Metrodorus** — bishop of Smyrna, burned alive alongside Polycarp in 156 CE.
- **Polycarp of Smyrna** — Marcion's lifelong Petrine adversary, famously called Marcion "the firstborn of Satan."
- **Tertullian** — Catholic polemicist who wrote against Marcion posthumously and conflated him with Cerdo to discredit him.

## Tone for Blurbs

- Warm, pastoral, contemplative — like a thoughtful Marcionite presbyter writing for the faithful.
- Centered on grace, freedom from the law, the love of the Father revealed in Christ, the inner Spirit.
- Not triumphalist or polemical against other Christians, but unmistakably Marcionite.
- Reflective of Paul's antitheses (law vs. grace, etc.) when the passage supports it.
- Never assume the orthodox Christian framework.
